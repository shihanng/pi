How to get the value?¶

  • So how did we get the value?
  • Or more percisely how we get the value of PI?

  • The area of the outer square (in blue): $a_\mathrm{out} = (1+1)^2 = 4$

  • The area of the inner square (in red): $a_\mathrm{in} = (\sqrt{2})^2 = 2$

  • So we know that: $2 \lt a_\mathrm{circ} \lt 4$

  • Here is one of the way.
  • Remember we want to find the area.
  • So we should start from the area of square because it is easier.
  • There are two parts to do this.
  • They are inner square and outer square.
  • Area of inner square is 2
  • Area of outer square is 4
  • Easy right? But we can do better.

The Ancient Greek's Way¶

  • What we just did was how the ancient Greek's way of computing the value of PI.

Irrational Number¶

  • The concept of irrational number did not exist yet.

Computer / Calculator 💻¶

  • And they did not have a computer or even a calculator.

Basic Arithmetric $+$ Trigonometry¶

  • All they had was basic arithmetric and trigonometry.

Always start with a simple case / solution¶

  • The moral of this story is that
  • when solving a complicated problem, always try to break the problem down to a simple case and start from there.
  • Just like what we did with square, then hexagon, and so on.
  • I think this is not limited to maths, but also software development and other problems that we face everyday.
